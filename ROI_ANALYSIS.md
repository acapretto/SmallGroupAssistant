## Project 1: Dynamic Partner Matcher

### The Idea
Real-time student pairing system that dynamically remixes student partnerships to compare answers and solve problems collaboratively. Eliminates wait time, keeps entire class engaged simultaneously.

### Question 1: Who Pays and How Many Exist?

**Paying Customer:** Middle/high school math teachers using active-learning pedagogy.

**Market Size:**
- US public + private K-12 schools: ~130,000
- High school math teachers: ~90,000
- Middle school math teachers: ~80,000
- Target (actively using collaborative learning): ~15,000–20,000 teachers
- Address (willing to pay): ~2,000–5,000 teachers (those already doing station rotation, peer learning)

**Reality check:** Andrew's 500 YouTube subscribers are mostly high school teachers, math enthusiasts, education tech followers. Maybe 50–100 are *actively in a classroom right now*. Of those, maybe 10–15 have explicit budget for tools beyond free Google Sheets. That's the starting addressable market.

### Question 2: Evidence of Demand

**Existing demand signals:**
- ✅ Teachers actively ask about "pairing strategies" in education Twitter, Reddit, TpT reviews
- ✅ Station-rotation and peer learning are well-established best practices (NCTM, edu research)
- ✅ Existing tools: Parlay (discussion platform), Nearpod (interactive lessons), but none specifically for dynamic micro-pairing
- ❓ Andrew has NOT received direct requests for this
- ❓ No search volume data (would need to check Google Trends)

**Verdict on demand:** Moderate. The pain point exists (bored students during peer review), but this is a "nice-to-have optimization" not a "must-have tool."

### Question 3: Realistic Revenue Path

**Pricing assumptions:**
- Price: $50–100/year per teacher (SaaS model) OR $15 one-time (TpT bundle)
- Andrew's audience: 500 YT subs, ~50 actively teaching
- Realistic conversion: 3–5 early adopters from YouTube audience
- Revenue in 6 months: 3–5 teachers × $50 = $150–250/year

**To reach $500/month (modest goal):**
- Need 100 paying teachers at $50/year OR 200 at $30/year
- Andrew's YT audience would have to grow 10x (5,000 subs) and convert at 2%
- **Realistic within 6 months?** No. Within 18–24 months with heavy promotion? Possible.

### Question 4: Honest Build Time

**MVP scope:** Session creator, pairing algorithm, student UI, teacher dashboard, basic data storage
- **Frontend (React):** 40–50 hours (pairing UI, timer, real-time updates)
- **Backend (Netlify Functions):** 20–30 hours (session management, pairing algorithm)
- **Testing & polish:** 20–30 hours
- **Documentation:** 10 hours
- **3x multiplier:** (40 + 20 + 20 + 10) × 1.5 = ~135 hours of actual work

**Realistic estimate:** 200–250 hours (5–6 weeks at 40 hrs/week)

### Question 5: Opportunity Cost

**Current active projects:** YouTube content, TpT store ($30/mo), vidprep, IEP tools, Future Problem Solving admin.

**What's at stake?**
- **YouTube channel:** Could release 1–2 strategic videos (lesson framework, competition math content) in those 6 weeks → audience growth, TpT sales lift
- **TpT store:** Could optimize product listings, create 2–3 new resource bundles → realistic $50–100/mo lift with ~20 hours
- **This project:** Builds 1 tool that starts at $0 revenue, takes 6 weeks to launch, needs audience growth to monetize

**Verdict:** Massive opportunity cost. 250 hours on Partner Matcher = 250 hours NOT on content, TpT optimization, or existing revenue streams.

### Question 6: Most Likely Failure Mode

Andrew builds partner-matcher MVP over 6 weeks. Launches to 500 YouTube subscribers. Gets 3 teachers to try it. They like it, but don't pay (prefer free Google Slides + manual timer). Andrew abandons it. Meanwhile, TpT store stagnates (no new content), YouTube channel goes quiet (no upload schedule), and zero revenue is generated. The tool sits on GitHub as "never completed."

### Scorecard

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Demand** | 4/10 | Nice-to-have optimization; no direct requests; search signal unknown. Pain point exists but isn't urgent. |
| **Income Potential** | 2/10 | Path to $500/mo requires 10x audience growth + conversion rate that's unrealistic for unknown tool. Likely $0–200/mo in first 6 months. |
| **Opportunity Cost** | 2/10 | 250 hours is enormous given current projects. TpT and YouTube are proven revenue channels being neglected. |
| **Audience Growth** | 5/10 | Niche appeal; would grow YouTube slightly if well-executed, but not a viral audience-builder. |
| **Aggregate** | **3.25/10** | (4×0.3 + 2×0.25 + 2×0.25 + 5×0.2) |

### **VERDICT: 🔴 KILL IT**

The math is clear: 250 hours to build a tool that has zero demand signals and a realistic revenue path of $0–200/month. Your TpT store could generate $100/month with 20 hours of optimization. Your YouTube channel is your long-term asset. Partner Matcher is a distraction.

**If you insist on validating:** Ask 20 teachers in your YouTube community: "Would you pay $50/year for a tool that auto-pairs students for peer review?" Record *yes*/*no*/*maybe* → only proceed if 7+ say yes. Time budget: 2 hours.

## Project 2: Gamified Points System

### The Idea
Replace traditional grading (90% = A) with open-ended point accumulation (10,000 points = master). Students earn through diverse pathways: tests, projects, videos, real-world applications. Flexibility + motivation.

### Question 1: Who Pays and How Many Exist?

**Paying Customer:** Math teachers + other subjects, wanting to move beyond traditional grading. Range from early-adopter progressives to traditional teachers seeking engagement strategies.

**Market Size:**
- US teachers: ~3.7 million
- Those actively seeking "alternative grading" or "competency-based" approaches: ~300,000–500,000
- Willing to pay for a tool: ~50,000–100,000 (institutional budgets, forward-thinking schools)
- Addressable (free-tier + paid): ~30,000

**Andrew's angle:** Teachers interested in engagement, FBM YouTube audience, TpT store followers. Again, ~50–100 actively teaching.

### Question 2: Evidence of Demand

**Demand signals:**
- ✅✅ STRONG: Alternative grading is a HUGEtrend (mastery-based learning, competency frameworks, standards-based grading)
- ✅✅ Teachers are ACTIVELY asking: "How do I move away from percentage grading?" (Twitter, Reddit, teacher forums)
- ✅ Existing competitors: Gradescope (for grading, not points), Schoology/Canvas (built-in grading), but few *points-based* systems
- ✅ Andrew himself is a teacher & frustrated with traditional grading (strong signal he understands the pain)
- ❓ Andrew has NOT received direct requests, but this is a known, large trend in education

**Verdict on demand:** STRONG. Alternative assessment is arguably the #1 conversation in K-12 education right now (standards-based, competency-based, portfolio). Teachers are hungry for this.

### Question 3: Realistic Revenue Path

**Pricing model:**
- Free tier: 1 class, basic features
- Paid: $10–20/month per teacher (SaaS model)
- OR $25 one-time (TpT bundle with template)

**Realistic revenue in 6 months:**
- YouTube reach: 500 subs, maybe 20 convert to free tier (4%)
- Free-to-paid conversion: 20% (realistic for education tools) = 4 paying teachers
- Revenue: 4 teachers × $15/month × 6 months = $360

**Path to $500/month:**
- Need ~35–40 paying teachers (at $15/mo)
- Conversion path: 1,000 YT views → 20 downloads → 4 paid = 0.4% paid conversion
- To hit 40 paid: need 10,000 views (doable with 2–3 strong videos + organic growth)
- Growth timeline: 3–6 months realistic

**Or:** Release on TpT as bundle ($15–25 each) → same audience reach but one-time sales spike.

### Question 4: Honest Build Time

**MVP scope:** Admin panel, activity setup, point entry, student dashboard, analytics
- **Frontend:** 60–80 hours (dashboards, point display, progress tracking)
- **Backend:** 30–40 hours (point logic, user auth, export)
- **Testing & polish:** 30 hours
- **Documentation:** 15 hours
- **3x multiplier:** (60 + 30 + 30 + 15) × 1.5 = ~210 hours

**Realistic estimate:** 220–280 hours (5–7 weeks)

### Question 5: Opportunity Cost

**What's at stake:**
- **TpT store:** Could create 1–2 "gamified grading" resource bundles (grade tracker sheets, point-tracking templates) → $50–150 revenue, ~30 hours
- **YouTube:** Could release 2–3 videos on "alternative grading strategies" (audience loves this) → audience lift, channel authority
- **This tool:** Generic web app (many competitors exist), needs marketing to stand out

**Unique advantage:** This is DIRECTLY relevant to Andrew's teacher audience. Unlike Partner Matcher (niche), gamified grading is a hot topic. YouTube videos about "move away from percentages" would attract viewers, who might then use the tool.

### Question 6: Most Likely Failure Mode

Andrew builds the gamified points tool, launches it, releases a YouTube video explaining the philosophy. Gets 15 free signups (3% of viewers). Zero paid conversions in month 1 (teachers don't value the tool enough to pay, prefer free Google Sheets + manual tracking). Discouraged, Andrew stops updating it. Tool sits dormant.

*Alternative failure:* Tool gets moderate traction (50 free users), but Andrew realizes he's now customer-support-bound (teachers emailing with issues) and hasn't accounted for maintenance burden. Abandons project after 2 months.

### Scorecard

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Demand** | 8/10 | Strong tailwinds: alternative grading is trending, teachers actively seeking solutions. This is a known, urgent pain point. |
| **Income Potential** | 6/10 | Path to $500/mo is realistic (40 teachers at $15/mo = 6 months). Could accelerate with YouTube + TpT synergy. |
| **Opportunity Cost** | 6/10 | 250 hours is significant, BUT this aligns with YouTube content + TpT store. Better ROI than Partner Matcher. |
| **Audience Growth** | 7/10 | Strong brand fit. "Alternative grading" videos would attract education-focused audience. Tool benefits from teacher educator brand. |
| **Aggregate** | **6.9/10** | (8×0.3 + 6×0.25 + 6×0.25 + 7×0.2) |

### **VERDICT: 🟢 BUILD IT** (with caution)

This is in the green zone. The demand is real, the revenue path is plausible, and it aligns with your brand. However, don't go all-in immediately.

**First step:** Release 1–2 YouTube videos explaining the philosophy ("Why Points Beat Percentages"). Measure engagement + comment requests. If you get 10+ comments like "How do I set this up?" → proceed to MVP. If crickets → kill it and focus on other content.

**Timeline:** 1-week validation (content + analytics), then 6-week build if green light.

## Project 3: Work Monitor AI

### The Idea
Real-time AI monitoring of student math work. Student uploads screenshot, Claude Vision detects errors in-progress, prompts student to verify (Socratic, not answer-giving). Responsive feedback without teacher intervention.

### Question 1: Who Pays and How Many Exist?

**Paying Customer:** Secondary math teachers, tutors, small group leaders who want real-time error-checking feedback without doing it manually.

**Market Size:**
- Math teachers: ~170,000 (K-12)
- Secondary (6-12): ~90,000
- Using AI-capable classrooms (laptops, tablets): ~40,000
- Addressable: ~5,000–10,000

**Andrew's niche:** Ideal for his audience (math education, tech-forward teachers, YouTube followers interested in AI). Maybe 20–30 are actually ready to adopt.

### Question 2: Evidence of Demand

**Demand signals:**
- ✅ Real problem: Teachers spend hours reviewing student work, marking errors
- ✅ Trend: AI in education is HUGE (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.); teachers are exploring applications
- ❓ Existing competition: Chegg's AI, some LMS plagiarism tools, but few *live error detection* tools
- ❌ Andrew has NOT received requests for this
- ❓ Search volume unknown

**Verdict:** Moderate-to-weak. The pain point is real, but it's a problem that doesn't have an existing "must-have" solution. Teachers might not realize they *need* this until they see it.

### Question 3: Realistic Revenue Path

**Pricing:**
- Free tier: 5 checks/month (teaser)
- Paid: $5–10/month per teacher (SaaS)
- OR embedded in a larger tool (partner with Desmos, GeoGebra, etc.)

**Revenue in 6 months:**
- YouTube reach: 500 subs, 2% convert (10 users)
- Free-to-paid: 10% (1–2 paying teachers)
- Revenue: 2 teachers × $7/mo × 6 mo = $84

**Path to $500/month:**
- Need ~70 paying teachers
- Requires significant marketing + partnership strategy (not just YouTube)
- Realistic timeline: 12–18 months, not 6

### Question 4: Honest Build Time

**MVP scope:** Screenshot upload, Claude Vision API, error detection, feedback UI, logging
- **Frontend (React + upload):** 30–40 hours
- **Backend (API wrapper for Claude Vision):** 20–25 hours
- **Claude prompting (getting error detection right):** 20–30 hours (iterating on prompts)
- **Testing:** 25 hours (edge cases: handwriting, multiple steps, different problem types)
- **Documentation:** 10 hours
- **3x multiplier:** (30 + 20 + 25 + 25 + 10) × 1.5 = ~195 hours

**Realistic estimate:** 200–250 hours (5–6 weeks)

BUT: Claude Vision for handwriting is unreliable. If students submit photos of written work (not typed), accuracy drops significantly. Would need to scope: "Typed math only" for MVP.

### Question 5: Opportunity Cost

**What's at stake:**
- **TpT:** Could create 3–4 "error-checking checkpoint" worksheets → $30–50 revenue, ~15 hours
- **YouTube:** Could release "AI for math teaching" content (trending!) → strong audience interest
- **This tool:** Niche + high technical risk (Claude Vision for math is hard)

**Technical risk:** This tool depends heavily on Claude Vision quality. If prompts don't work well, the entire tool collapses. You'd spend 50+ hours debugging why feedback is wrong.

### Question 6: Most Likely Failure Mode

Andrew spends 6 weeks building. Releases MVP. Uploads a work sample: "2x + 5 = 13, so x = 8." Claude correctly identifies error (x = 4). Andrew feels confident. Scales testing to 10 more problems. Claude misses 2 of them, or gives confusing feedback. Andrew realizes tuning Claude Vision for math is harder than expected. Another 30+ hours of prompt engineering. Meanwhile, no users have adopted it. Project abandoned.

### Scorecard

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Demand** | 5/10 | Real pain point, but unproven. Teachers don't know they need it yet. No direct requests. |
| **Income Potential** | 2/10 | Path to revenue requires significant marketing + partnerships. Realistic: $0–100/mo in 6 months. |
| **Opportunity Cost** | 3/10 | 250 hours + high technical risk. Could derail other projects if debugging takes longer. |
| **Audience Growth** | 6/10 | "AI for teaching" is hot; content could drive viewers. But the tool itself isn't naturally viral. |
| **Aggregate** | **4.05/10** | (5×0.3 + 2×0.25 + 3×0.25 + 6×0.2) |

### **VERDICT: 🟡 VALIDATE FIRST**

The idea is interesting but unproven. Before building the full MVP, validate the critical assumption: **Does Claude Vision actually work well for detecting math errors?**

**Validation experiment:**
- **Test:** Create 20 handwritten + 20 typed math problems with intentional errors. Upload to Claude Vision, measure accuracy.
- **Method:** Batch test using Claude Opus (best vision performance). Document error types.
- **Time budget:** 3 hours
- **Success criteria:** >80% accuracy on typed, >70% on handwriting → proceed
- **Kill criteria:** <70% accuracy overall → not ready, kill project
